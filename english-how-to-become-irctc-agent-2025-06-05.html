What is an IRCTC Agent and Why is it Important for You?

An IRCTC (Indian Railway Catering and Tourism Corporation) Authorized Agent is an individual or business officially appointed and certified by IRCTC to book online train e-tickets for passengers. This is completely different from a personal User ID. An official IRCTC Agent ID provides you with the following facilities:

  • Ability to legally book an unlimited number of train tickets.
  • Access to a special booking portal for agents (IRCTC agent login), which may include enhanced features and sometimes special quotas.
  • Opportunity to provide other value-added services to customers besides ticket booking.
  • Operation of a recognized and reliable railway ticket booking business.

Eligibility Criteria and Required Documents to Become an IRCTC Agent

Generally, the eligibility criteria are straightforward and simple. To become an IRCTC agent, you must meet the following criteria and submit documents:

  • Applicant must be an Indian citizen.
  • Minimum age (usually 18 years).
  • Basic knowledge of computer and internet.
  • A valid email ID and mobile number, not previously registered with IRCTC for any personal or agent account.
  • Proof of address for your shop/office or residence (if the business is operated from home).
  • Class 3rd Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is mandatory.

IRCTC Tatkal Booking Rules for 2025 and Impact on Agents

IRCTC has announced significant changes to its policies for 2025, particularly aimed at streamlining the Tatkal ticket booking process and reducing misuse.

  • Mandatory Aadhaar Authentication and OTP Verification: From July 1, 2025, Aadhaar authentication will be mandatory for passengers booking Tatkal tickets via the IRCTC website or app. From July 15, 2025, all Tatkal bookings (online, at PRS counters, or through authorized agents) will require One-Time Password (OTP) verification sent to the Aadhaar-linked mobile number.
  • New Restrictions on Agents During Tatkal Booking: To prevent misuse and prioritize genuine users, ticketing agents will be blocked from Tatkal bookings during peak demand periods. This restriction will be from 10:00 AM to 10:30 AM for AC Class and from 11:00 AM to 11:30 AM for Non-AC Class. Agents can only book Tatkal tickets after this window closes.

These policy changes significantly impact the booking process for Ftech Global's agents. Ftech Global comprehensively trains its agents on these new rules and procedures, enabling them to adapt to these changes and operate their business legally and efficiently.
